# CS-330
Comp Graphic and Visualization

How do I approach designing software?

I approach designing software by first figuring out what the final product needs to do and what the user should experience. For this project, that meant looking at my Trek image and breaking it down into the main pieces: ships, planet, starfield, photon torpedoes, lighting, textures, and camera movement. The project helped me get better at designing in 3D space, especially with scale, object placement, and how lighting affects the overall mood. My process was very iterative. I built the scene in stages, tested how it looked, and kept making adjustments until the objects felt like they belonged together. I can apply that same design approach in the future by breaking larger projects into smaller parts and improving them one step at a time.

How do I approach developing programs?

I approach developing programs by getting a working version built first, then refining it through testing and feedback. With this 3D scene, I focused on making sure the basic objects, camera, and lighting worked before trying to polish the details. One development strategy I used more in this project was organizing the code into reusable functions for drawing ships, setting transformations, applying textures, and configuring lights. Iteration was a huge part of the project because several pieces needed multiple passes, especially the Klingon ships, photon torpedoes, and overall scene layout. Throughout the milestones, I became more comfortable moving from small tasks to a complete scene made of connected objects.

How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science helps me reach my goals by giving me stronger problem-solving skills and a better understanding of how the digital world functions. Computational graphics helped me see how programming, math, and visual design work together. Skills like transformations, lighting, textures, camera control, and modular code will help me in future courses because they require both technical thinking and creativity. Professionally, these skills could apply to software development, simulations, visual tools, game development, or any field where users need to interact with digital information. It is my hope, even at my age, that I can make a career change I very much desire. This project, and this course, were a lot of fun, but even something fun still requires a lot of planning, structure and debugging to get it where I'm satisfied with the end result.
